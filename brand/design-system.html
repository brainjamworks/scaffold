Foundations

Design Principles

Six rules that guide every visual decision. When two principles conflict, the lower-numbered one wins.

01

Calm + Considered

Authors are building real teaching content. The editor is a focused work surface, not a marketing site, not a slide deck, not a kid's app.

02

Education-flavoured

Reads as a serious tool for educators. Triple-colour palette is the fingerprint. Block-stack mark is the literal product metaphor.

03

Functional First

Every element earns its place. No decoration for decoration's sake. If it doesn't help the author understand or act, remove it.

04

Surface-aware

Editor surfaces are light + spacious + ink-on-canvas. Brand-presence surfaces (splash, login, marketing) are dark + bold + confident.

05

Restraint with Colour

Mostly ink + canvas. Navy carries brand. Coral = destructive/error only. Teal = correctness/affirmation only. No gradient, ever.

06

Distinct, not Derivative

Not Linear, not Notion, not Canvas, not Teachable, not Learnhouse. The triple palette + register mark + Poppins / JetBrains Mono pairing make scaffold recognisable on its own.

Brand

Logo & Mark

The register mark is four construction corners framing a bold plus — the dashed add-block slot reduced to its corners. The framed-but-empty slot reads as "add the next block"; the plus is the figure, the corners the ground. One hue throughout so it reduces cleanly to a favicon and single-ink stamps. The wordmark uses Poppins 700 with the dot in coral on light + dark surfaces, teal on navy.

Do
  • Use the register mark — four dashed corners framing a bold plus
  • Keep the plus centred and dominant; keep the corners quiet
  • One hue: navy on light, white on dark / navy surfaces
  • Use the coral dot in the wordmark on light + dark, teal on navy
  • Use the small-variant mark (solid corners) below 22px
  • Maintain clear space ≥ the plus's half-height around the mark
Don't
  • Close the corners into a full box — the open, framed slot is the point
  • Add a gradient to the mark or wordmark — gradient is dead
  • Tint the plus with coral or teal — they're reserved semantic signals
  • Use the dashed mark at sub-22px sizes — use the small variant (solid corners)
  • Apply effects (drop shadow, outline, glow)
  • Place on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
Voice

Writing Style

Direct, calm, educator-respecting. No exclamation marks, no emoji in chrome, no consultant-speak. Always specific over abstract.

Be specific, not vague
"3 hotspots · partial credit"
"Customize your assessment experience!"
Direct over flowery
"Add a hint"
"✨ Empower your learners with helpful hints"
Educator, not marketer
"This block is graded."
"Unlock powerful grading features."
Quiet errors
"Image URL not valid."
"Oops! Something went wrong 😬"
Empty states with purpose
"Start with a block. Type / for the library."
"Nothing here yet."
Lowercase for chrome
"draft · 5 pts · graded"
"DRAFT · 5 PTS · GRADED"
